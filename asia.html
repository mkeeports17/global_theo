Christianity in Asia

  • 60% of the global population lives in asia, but only 8.2% are christians
  • As of 2020, Asia’s leading religion is Muslim, with 27.4% of all asians.
  • Also as of 2020, 640 million people in the continent reported having no religion, which is just under 14% of the population.

Christians in Asia mainly stick to the method of religious theology. The reason is because the continent of Asia has a lot of different religions. This methodology is a way of doing theology by seeking to understand and express christian faith within the contexts dominated by non-christian religions. For example, Christians in Asia that would like to evangelize to their neighbors will need to understand their neighbor’s religion to explain to them the differences between it and Christianity. Then they can talk about why Christianity’s points of belief are more applicable than the other religions.

Asia was the most nonreligious continent in the 20th century, which we assume was because of the spread of communist regimes prohibiting religions. Anglicans and Orthodox churches have failed miserably in Asia but the fastest current growth rates of Christian denominations are Catholics and Protestants, and are found most in South Asia and Southeastern Asia. Mongolia, Nepal, and Cambodia are three nations that have increasing Christian rates, primarily due to vigorous missions and loose state restrictions. Research showed in 2020 that 56 million Christians worship in house churches in Asia. House churches are common in China because of the restrictions against practicing publicly. Christianity in Asia has been increasing nonetheless in every area except Western Asia, decreasing by 23% from 1900 to 2020. Some Christian populations have suffered in these areas because of violent religious extremism and military involvement.
