Christians by Continent, 1910 and 2010

The pie chart to the right shows the proportion of Christians in the world that lived in each continent at that time.

Over the past century, global Christianity has undergone a significant demographic shift, with the majority of Christians now residing in the global South rather than the global North. While Christians have consistently made up about one-third of the worldâ€™s population, the geographic distribution has changed dramatically; in 1910, 66% of Christians lived in Europe, compared to only 25.6% by 2010, while Africa's Christian population grew from less than 2% to nearly 22% in the same period.

Christians by Continent, 2020

The map to the left shows the proportion of Christians in the world that lived in each continent at that time.

Today, 67% of Christians live in the global South, a shift accompanied by notable differences in worldview between Northern and Southern Christians. Despite these demographic changes, Christianity's cultural adaptability, rooted in its history of translation and its origins in a non-European context, remains one of its defining strengths. This adaptability will be crucial as the faith continues to evolve within diverse cultural settings, echoing the transformative shifts seen in its earliest days. This site will highlight three of the four continents with the most Christians, each of the three being in the global south.
